The Core & The Computational

Foundational pillars like Number Theory and Topology continue to pose deep questions, while computational mathematics provides powerful new tools for both theoretical and applied problems.

P versus NP

A central problem in computational complexity. It asks: if the answer to a problem is easy to verify, is it also easy to solve? The answer could revolutionize computing and cryptography.

Riemann Hypothesis

A conjecture about the locations of zeros of the Riemann zeta function. Its proof would have profound consequences for our understanding of the distribution of prime numbers.

The Quantum & AI Revolutions

Two of the most powerful forces reshaping science are deeply rooted in mathematics. Quantum mechanics requires new mathematical languages, while AI is becoming a collaborator in mathematical discovery itself.

The AI-Mathematics Symbiosis

AI is evolving from a mere tool to a genuine partner in research, creating a powerful cycle of discovery.

1. Human Intuition & Guidance

Mathematicians identify promising areas and pose high-level questions.

2. AI Conjecture Generation

Machine learning models analyze data to find hidden patterns and propose new hypotheses.

3. Formalization & Verification

AI "copilots" help translate ideas into rigorous, machine-readable proofs in systems like Lean.

4. New Theorems & Deeper Understanding

This collaborative cycle accelerates the pace of discovery and formalization.
